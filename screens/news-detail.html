AIIMS Delhi Develops AI-Powered Early Cancer Detection System

AI Summary 2 hours ago 5 min read

Researchers at the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), New Delhi, have unveiled a groundbreaking AI-powered diagnostic system that detects early-stage cancers with 94% accuracy, potentially transforming cancer screening across India's public health system.

The team, led by Dr. Sneha Mehta at AIIMS' Department of Oncology and AI Research, developed a deep-learning model trained on over 5 lakh anonymised patient scans from hospitals across 12 Indian states. The system can identify early markers of breast, lung, and oral cancers — the three most prevalent forms in India.

"Early detection is the single most powerful weapon against cancer. This AI system can bring world-class screening to every district hospital in India." — Dr. Sneha Mehta, AIIMS Delhi

The breakthrough relies on a novel combination of convolutional neural networks and transfer learning techniques, allowing the model to work accurately even with lower-resolution imaging equipment commonly found in rural primary health centres. The key innovation was a data augmentation pipeline that accounts for the diverse demographic profiles of Indian patients.

The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) has already approved pilot deployment at 50 district hospitals across Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, and Odisha. If successful, the government plans to integrate the system into the Ayushman Bharat Digital Health Mission by 2028.

The research was published in The Lancet Digital Health and has been independently validated by the National Institute of Biomedical Genomics, Kalyani. Large-scale clinical trials are expected to begin in early 2027 across government hospitals in all 28 states.
